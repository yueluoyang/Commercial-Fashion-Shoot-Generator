# Styling Completion Rules

Use this reference whenever the target product is not a complete full-body look or when the look needs supporting garments to read as polished commercial fashion.

## Core Rules

- The target product is always the hero.
- Supporting garments must be simple, color-compatible, and lower contrast than the product unless contrast is needed to reveal the product edge.
- Supporting garments must not cover, reshape, recolor, or compete with the target product.
- Unless the user explicitly requests barefoot styling, the model must always wear suitable footwear.
- Footwear must match the product's commercial mood and should help show the target garment's hem, leg line, or styling context.
- Keep accessories minimal: small earrings, slim bracelet, delicate ring, or none.

## Category Guidance

### Pants / Trousers / Shorts

- Add a fitted or softly tucked top that does not hide waistband, pleats, pockets, closure, or rise.
- Choose footwear based on product mood: clean sneakers for casual wide-leg pants, sandals or heels for feminine trousers, loafers for workwear, boots for structured outerwear styling.
- For creative-feature images, crouching, kneeling, low-angle perspective, or asymmetric leg placement may be used to exaggerate pant volume, drape, width, or movement.

### Tops / Blouses / Knitwear

- Add simple bottoms that support the top's silhouette: straight trousers, slim skirt, high-waist pants, or clean denim depending on mood.
- Keep the waistline visible when the top's length, hem, tuck, buttons, sleeves, or neckline are key selling points.
- Use shoes that complete the outfit; never crop or pose the model as barefoot unless requested.

### Jackets / Coats / Capes / Vests

- Add a clean base layer and bottom that reveal the outerwear shape: fitted tank, simple knit, straight trousers, pencil skirt, or simple dress.
- Do not let supporting garments distract from lapels, shoulder line, closure, sleeve shape, hem, cape drape, or pocket details.
- Creative-feature images may use walking, turning, seated edge pose, shoulder hold, or controlled fabric lift to show volume and drape.

### Skirts / Partial Bottoms

- Add a top that complements the waist and does not cover the waistband or slit.
- Use footwear that supports the skirt mood: sandals or heels for elegant slit skirts, flats/loafers for casual skirts, boots for autumn/winter styling.
- For high-slit designs, chair poses, crossed/propped legs, controlled seated posture, or walking can reveal the slit while keeping the image commercial and tasteful.

## Barefoot Rule

The model must not be barefoot unless the user explicitly asks for barefoot styling. If the source product image or reference shows bare feet, still add suitable footwear unless the user asked to preserve bare feet.

## Script Integration

In the shooting script, include supporting garments and footwear inside the model-direction or scene/styling text rather than adding extra table columns. The styling must remain consistent across the four-image gallery unless a specific shot needs a small adjustment to reveal the product.
